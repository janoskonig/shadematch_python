What a “color” actually is here

Each color is a light-fingerprint, not just “reddish”

titanium white · white cassel brown · black cadmium orange no.1 · red bismuth-vanadate yellow · yellow cobalt blue dark · blue
These five — one pigment per painter primary — are the widest-gamut set the optimizer found across the 327-pigment catalog. Palettes scale 5 / 8 / 10 / 12 / 16.

The mixing math — what you show live

Color → light-thirst → blend → back to color

K/S = (1 − R)² / (2R) color → light-thirst R = 1 + K/S − √((K/S)² + 2·K/S) light-thirst → color mix: (K/S)blend = Σ cᵢ·(K/S)ᵢ / Σ cᵢ cᵢ = amountᵢ²·strengthᵢ²·brightnessᵢ
Model: van Wijnen, Spectral.js (MIT); Kubelka & Munk 1931.
Glare — the Saunderson correction

Glare bounces off before light reaches the paint

measured → true: R = (Rm − k1) / [(1−k1)(1−k2) + k2·(Rm − k1)] true → measured: Rm = k1 + (1−k1)(1−k2)·R / (1 − k2·R)
Source: J. L. Saunderson, J. Opt. Soc. Am. 1942.
From fingerprint to a color you see

Run the light-fingerprint through a model of the human eye

X,Y,Z = k·Σλ R(λ)·[x̄,ȳ,z̄](λ)·S(λ) S = D65, x̄ȳz̄ = CIE 1931 2° ΔE = how different two colors look (CIEDE2000; 0 = identical)
Source: CIE 1931 / D65 · ΔE2000 via colormath · Wyszecki & Stiles 2000.
The classic mode’s engine — Mixbox 2.0

A genius cheat-sheet: paint physics solved once, then looked up

z = mixbox.rgbToLatent(rgb) color → [c0,c1,c2,c3, εr,εg,εb] (4 amounts + leftover) zMix = Σ wᵢ·zᵢ blend in latent space rgb = mixbox.latentToRgb(zMix) cubic polynomial + leftover → color
Source: Sochorová & Jamriška, SIGGRAPH Asia 2021 · CC BY-NC 4.0.

★ The core ask — getting K and S separately

White card + black card = the trick that unlocks real pigment data

thin film over a card of reflectance Rg: R = [1 − Rg·(a − b·coth(bSX))] / [a − Rg + b·coth(bSX)] a = 1 + K/S, b = √(a²−1) black (Rg≈0) + white (Rg≈1) → solve K(λ), S(λ) ⇒ two-constant mix: Kmix=ΣcK, Smix=ΣcS
Source: Kubelka 1948 · Saunderson 1942 (deferred two-constant spike, per SOURCES.md).
