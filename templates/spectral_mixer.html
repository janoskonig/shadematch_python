Two-flux finite layersynthetic

Renders your mix as a finite film over a backing instead of a fully opaque masstone — so thin layers let the backing show through (hiding power). The math is the real Kubelka–Munk two-flux solution driven by the measured K/S, but the scattering coefficient S is assumed flat (we have no black/white drawdown spectra yet), so “opacity” is a synthetic stand-in for film thickness, not a measured value. Full opacity = the standard engine.
